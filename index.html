Why CivicTrack

Built for Real Civic Impact

Getting Started

How CivicTrack Works

Every account can move between three role-based workspaces. Pick a role below to see exactly what you'll do, step by step.

1

Choose your role

Open the app and select Citizen from the global role switcher on the home screen.

2

Report an issue

Tap New Complaint, then type or speak the problem. The AI assistant turns your words into a formal complaint, geo-tags it, and saves it instantly — even offline.

3

Track progress

Follow your complaint through Submitted → Assigned → Under Review → In Progress → Resolved from the live Grievance Redressal grid.

4

Pay bills & book services

Settle property tax with a Razorpay QR scan, reserve a community hall, or apply for a certificate — all from the Citizen tab.

5

Participate & earn XP

Vote in local development polls, post in the community forum, and build toward badges and transport-discount rewards.

1

Switch to Worker mode

Use the role switcher to open the field-worker workspace.

2

Claim a task

Browse the live Task Dispatch Board for nearby unresolved issues, sorted by location and urgency, and claim one.

3

Resolve & document

Mark the task In Progress, complete the work, then upload photo proof and notes through the Instant Resolution Scanner.

4

Get paid & rated

Once verified, a mock payout is issued automatically. Citizen ratings feed your Performance Analytics and Worker XP.

5

Stay safe & coordinated

Check in via Worker Safety Check-in on hazardous jobs and stay connected with your crew through Team Chat & Radios.

1

Open the live dashboard

Monitor incoming complaints, ward environmental metrics, and worker availability in one view.

2

Assign work

Route pending issues to the right field worker based on location and current workload.

3

Communicate with the ward

Publish notices, post tenders for bids, and broadcast emergency alerts when conditions demand it.

4

Review & improve

Use Citizen Feedback Analytics and the Worker Performance Leaderboard to keep service quality high — every action is recorded in the Security & Audit Trail.

Under the Hood

Architecture & Tech Stack

100% Kotlin, built on Jetpack Compose, with a local-first data layer and a thin AI client that degrades gracefully when offline.

graph TD
    A[MainActivity] --> B[MyApplicationTheme]
    B --> C[MainWorkspace Composable]
    C --> D[CivicViewModel]
    D --> E[CivicRepository]
    E --> F[Room Database: SQLite]
    E --> G[Gemini AI Client: Retrofit and Moshi]
    D --> H[Realtime StateFlows]
    H --> I[Dashboard / Services / Complaints / AI / Polls / Hub]
complaints

Title, description, category, status (Submitted → Resolved), priority, location, photo tags, resolution notes, and citizen ratings.

notices

Local announcements, tender targets, ward boundaries, and emergency alerts.

projects

Municipal development initiatives with progress meters, budget limits, and contracting parties.

polls

Local governance surveys with options, vote totals, and citizen-voted tags.

carbon_metrics

Daily CO₂ emissions across grid, waste, transport, and water against municipal thresholds.

app_config

System configuration, user XP points, and unlocked badges.

Run It Locally

CivicTrack runs fully offline out of the box — a Gemini API key only unlocks live AI responses.

1

Clone the repository

git clone https://github.com/karan942006/CivicTrack-2.0.git
2

Open in Android Studio

Open the project folder and let Gradle finish syncing dependencies.

3

(Optional) Add a Gemini API key

Add your key to local.properties to enable live AI drafting and summarization. Skip this step and the app falls back to realistic simulated AI responses automatically.

GEMINI_API_KEY=your_key_here
4

Build & run

Select an Android device or emulator and hit Run. The Room database initializes with sample ward data on first launch.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does CivicTrack work without internet?

Yes. Every core flow is backed by a local Room (SQLite) database. If there's no API key or active connection, AI features fall back to realistic simulated responses instead of failing.

Which platforms are supported?

CivicTrack 2.0 is a native Android app built entirely in Kotlin with Jetpack Compose. There's currently no iOS or web client.

How do I switch between Citizen, Worker, and Admin?

Use the global role switcher inside the app to move instantly between workspaces — no separate logins or app installs required.

Are the bill payments and payouts real?

No. Razorpay QR payments and worker payouts are mocked for demonstration purposes — no real money moves through the app.

Can I contribute or report a bug?

Yes — the project is open source. Open an issue or pull request on the GitHub repository, or use the feedback form below.
